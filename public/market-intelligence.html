Seasonality
Seasonal Price Patterns
Average historical price patterns help you time your buying and selling decisions.
Feeder Cattle: Typically peak in March-April (spring demand) and September-October (fall weaning runs). Prices usually bottom in January-February (winter feeding costs) and November-December (heavy fall run supply).
Fed Cattle: Strongest in April-May (grilling season ramp-up) and weakest in November-January. Holiday retail demand for beef cuts peaks in May-July.
Cull Cows: Highest prices in March-April (tight supply after winter) and lowest in October-November (fall liquidation). Lean beef demand for hamburger supports spring prices.
